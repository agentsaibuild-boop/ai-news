## ⭐ Top Story

**TSMC posted a record quarter — and June was the biggest month in its nearly four-decade history.** The world's largest contract chipmaker reported Monday that Q2 revenue hit **NT$1.27 trillion (~$39.62 billion), up 36% year-over-year**, near the top of its guidance, powered by AI-chip demand from Nvidia, Apple and the rest of the accelerator complex. ## 💼 Business & Market

- **Anthropic launched Claude for Teachers — free premium Claude for US K-12 educators.** Announced Tuesday, verified teachers who sign up by June 30, 2027 get a **full year of premium access**, lesson planning aligned to **academic standards in all 50 states** (via a "Learning Commons" layer), trusted curricula like OpenSciEd and Illustrative Mathematics, and **nine education connectors** (MagicSchool, Canva Education, Brisk, TeachFX and more). ## 🧠 AI Fun Fact of the Day

> The first computer program to beat a reigning human world champion at any game did it 47 years ago this month: in July 1979 in Monte Carlo, Hans Berliner's **BKG 9.8** defeated backgammon world champion Luigi Villa **7–1** for a $5,000 winner-take-all prize. Berliner, a CMU professor, later admitted the program rolled luckier dice — but his analysis showed it also made no serious mistakes while the human did.
