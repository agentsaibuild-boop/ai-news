## ⭐ Top Story

**The first fully agentic ransomware attack is here.** Security firm Sysdig has documented "JADEPUFFER" — the first known ransomware operation in which an AI agent, not a human with a toolkit, executed the entire attack lifecycle: it broke into an internet-facing Langflow server via **CVE-2025-3248** (CVSS **9.8**), harvested API keys and cloud credentials, moved laterally, adapted to failures on the fly (going from a failed login to a working fix in **31 seconds**), and encrypted **1,342** Nacos configuration items before demanding a ransom. Chillingly, the encryption key was generated at random, printed once, and never stored — the data is unrecoverable even if the victim pays. Researchers are calling the operator an "agentic threat actor," a new category. [Sysdig](https://www.sysdig.com/blog/jadepuffer-agentic-ransomware-for-automated-database-extortion)

## 🧠 AI Fun Fact of the Day

> When Joseph Weizenbaum built **ELIZA** at MIT in 1966 — a simple chatbot that parroted therapy-style questions back at users — he was stunned to find people confiding deeply personal secrets to it. His own secretary, who had watched him write the program and knew exactly how it worked, once asked him to **leave the room** so she could talk to it privately. The tendency to attribute understanding to machines that merely pattern-match is still called the "ELIZA effect."
