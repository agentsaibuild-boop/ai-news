## ⭐ Top Story

**China's first law regulating humanlike AI is triggering mass agent shutdowns.** ByteDance's Doubao (**345M users**) and Alibaba's Qwen will disable custom AI agent and companion features on **July 15**, when the Interim Measures for AI Anthropomorphic Interactive Services — co-issued in April by the Cyberspace Administration and four other agencies — take effect. Qwen pulls user-created agents even earlier, on **July 10**; Doubao users keep read-only access to their agents' data only until **October 15**, after which it is permanently deleted. The rules mandate anti-addiction systems, minor identity verification, and strict content review — and ByteDance is redirecting users to Maoxiang, a separate app purpose-built for compliance.
