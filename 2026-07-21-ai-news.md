## 🏛️ Policy, Regulation & Governance

- **The EU ordered Google to open Android to rival AI assistants — and share its search data.** In binding Digital Markets Act decisions issued Wednesday, the Commission requires Google to give competing AI assistants access to **11 Android feature groups** (voice-activated tasks like booking taxis and replying to messages), let users summon a third-party assistant by voice the way "Hey Google" works today, and share anonymized ranking, query, click and view data with qualifying search engines and AI chatbots from **January 2027**. Android interoperability lands with Android 18 by **August 2027**; concurrent wake-words by 2028. Google says the order creates privacy and security risks. [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/07/16/eu-orders-google-to-share-search-data-open-android-to-ai-rivals-competitors) · [TechRepublic](https://www.techrepublic.com/article/news-eu-google-android-ai-search-data-rules-emea/)
- **The Commission published its Article 50 transparency guidelines — 13 days before the rules bite.** Adopted Monday, the guidelines spell out how AI providers and deployers must tell users they're interacting with an AI system, machine-readably mark generative-AI output, and disclose deepfakes and emotion-recognition systems. The underlying AI Act transparency obligations apply from **August 2, 2026**. [European Commission](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/library/guidelines-transparency-obligations-providers-and-deployers-ai-systems)

## 🔬 Research & Science

- **AI text detectors collapse when models imitate an author's style, Epoch AI found.** Pangram, GPTZero and Originality.ai catch plain AI text almost perfectly (worst false-negative rate: 0.7%). But when Claude Opus 4.8, GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro were given five writing samples from a real author and asked to mimic the style, an average **13%** of passages slipped through — and in scientific writing, **24–29%** went undetected, with one detector missing 48% of Gemini-written academic passages. [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/ai-text-detectors-struggle-when-language-models-mimic-an-authors-style/)

## 🧠 AI Fun Fact of the Day

> Next-token prediction is older than computers: in 1913, Russian mathematician Andrey Markov hand-counted **20,000 letters** of Pushkin's *Eugene Onegin*, tallying how vowels and consonants follow each other to prove that dependent events could be analyzed statistically. The "Markov chains" he invented with pen and paper are the direct mathematical ancestors of every modern language model.
