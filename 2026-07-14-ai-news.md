## 🏛️ Policy, Regulation & Governance

- **China's AI-companion rules bite tomorrow — and user data starts disappearing.** The *Interim Measures for AI Anthropomorphic Interactive Services* (issued in April by the Cyberspace Administration and four other agencies) take effect **July 15**, forcing ByteDance's **Doubao** and Alibaba's **Qwen** apps to shut down personalized "humanlike" AI agents. Doubao users keep read-only access to their agents and chat histories until **October 15**; Qwen users get no grace period — Alibaba says configurations and conversations will be **permanently deleted**, with no migration path. [TechNode](https://technode.com/2026/07/06/bytedances-doubao-and-alibabas-qwen-to-shut-down-ai-agent-features-on-july-15/) · [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/tech/big-tech/article/3359482/bytedance-and-alibaba-disable-humanlike-ai-custom-agents-new-rules-loom)
- **The New York Times wants OpenAI sanctioned for hiding evidence.** In a motion filed last Thursday (July 9), the Times, the New York Daily News and 15 other publishers accused OpenAI of falsely telling the court it couldn't search its models for copyrighted material — while allegedly having done exactly that "even before the first News Plaintiff filed suit" — and of deleting or making unsearchable **billions of ChatGPT conversations**. OpenAI denies the claims and calls the motion an attempt to "invade the privacy" of users. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/09/new-york-times-says-openai-hid-evidence-in-chatgpt-copyright-trial/) · [US News/Reuters](https://money.usnews.com/investing/news/articles/2026-07-09/new-york-times-led-group-asks-court-to-sanction-openai-in-us-copyright-dispute)

## 🔬 Research & Science

- **Ant Group open-sourced guardrail models built specifically for autonomous agents.** Announced Monday, **SingGuard-NSFA** screens an agent's intended actions *before execution* for prompt injection, sensitive-data theft, malicious code execution, resource abuse and permission misuse — covering **7 risk categories, 185 scenarios and 133 languages**, with a ~**50 ms** risk judgment. ## 🧠 AI Fun Fact of the Day

> The most famous "AI" of the 18th century was a fraud with a person inside: the **Mechanical Turk** (1770), a chess-playing "automaton" that toured Europe for decades and beat Napoleon Bonaparte and Benjamin Franklin — its cabinet secretly concealed a human chess master. Amazon later named its crowdsourcing platform "Mechanical Turk" after the hoax, cheerfully describing the service as "artificial artificial intelligence."
