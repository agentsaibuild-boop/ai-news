## ⭐ Top Story

**Apple is suing OpenAI for trade secret theft.** In a lawsuit filed Friday in federal court in Northern California, Apple alleges that OpenAI has been stealing its confidential information "at every level, from members of its Technical Staff to its Chief Hardware Officer" to build its upcoming AI hardware. The complaint claims OpenAI hardware chief **Tang Tan** — himself a former Apple VP — directed job candidates still working at Apple to bring "actual parts" to interviews for "show and tell" sessions, and that another departing engineer downloaded dozens of confidential files and took an Apple laptop. More than **400 former Apple employees** now work at OpenAI, many recruited after OpenAI's **$6.4 billion** acquisition of Jony Ive's io Products put it on a collision course with its onetime iPhone partner. OpenAI's response: "We have no interest in other companies' trade secrets." [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html)

## 🧠 AI Fun Fact of the Day

> The first AI program ever written got rejected by an academic journal — sort of. In 1956, the **Logic Theorist** by Allen Newell, Herbert Simon, and Cliff Shaw proved 38 theorems from *Principia Mathematica*, and for theorem 2.85 it found a proof **more elegant than Bertrand Russell's original** (Russell was delighted). But when the authors submitted a paper listing Logic Theorist as a co-author, the *Journal of Symbolic Logic* declined it — the result was already known, and the journal wasn't ready for a machine on the byline.
