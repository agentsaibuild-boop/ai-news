## ⭐ Top Story

**Apple is suing OpenAI for trade secret theft.** In a lawsuit filed Friday in federal court in Northern California, Apple alleges that OpenAI has been stealing its confidential information "at every level, from members of its Technical Staff to its Chief Hardware Officer" to build its upcoming AI hardware. The complaint claims OpenAI hardware chief **Tang Tan** — himself a former Apple VP — directed job candidates still working at Apple to bring "actual parts" to interviews for "show and tell" sessions, and that another departing engineer downloaded dozens of confidential files and took an Apple laptop. More than **400 former Apple employees** now work at OpenAI, many recruited after OpenAI's **$6.4 billion** acquisition of Jony Ive's io Products put it on a collision course with its onetime iPhone partner. OpenAI's response: "We have no interest in other companies' trade secrets." [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html)

## 🏛️ Policy, Regulation & Governance

- **The EU AI Act's transparency regime is entering its endgame.** Article 50 obligations — mandatory disclosure for chatbots, labeling of deepfakes and AI-generated content — become enforceable across all 27 member states on **August 2**, with fines up to **€15 million or 3% of global turnover**. Companies that want a presumption of conformity can sign the AI Office's new **Code of Practice on Transparency of AI-Generated Content by July 22** (18:00 CET); a pending omnibus package may push the provider-marking deadline to December 2, but the chatbot and deepfake rules stand. [TechPolicy.Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/the-eus-ai-transparency-code-of-practice-explained/) · [European Commission](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/code-practice-ai-generated-content)
- **The Federal Reserve put Marc Andreessen in charge of thinking about AI and jobs.** Fed Chair Kevin Warsh announced five external task forces on July 9; the a16z co-founder will co-lead the Productivity and Jobs panel alongside Stanford economist **Charles I. Jones**, examining how AI reshapes productivity, employment, and monetary policy. Recommendations are due by the **end of 2026** — and if the panel concludes AI productivity gains are real and large, it could change how the Fed models potential GDP growth. [The Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/07/09/federal-reserve-enlists-marc-andreessen-advise-ai-under-warsh/)

## 🔬 Research & Science

- **OpenAI says GPT-5.6 "Sol Ultra" proved a 50-year-old open math conjecture in under an hour.** On Friday the company published a proof of the **Cycle Double Cover Conjecture** — a famous graph-theory problem open since the 1970s — generated by its top-end Sol Ultra tier using **64 parallel subagents**, and released the full proof PDF and prompt. Early expert reaction is a study in nuance: mathematician **Thomas Bloom** calls it "a very nice proof" but notes it is short, elementary, leans on techniques from a **1983 paper it never cites**, and "could have been discovered in the 1980s" — recombination more than revolution. The proof has not been formalized in a proof assistant, and the community is still checking it; several past claimed proofs of this conjecture later collapsed.
